Meaning of Walk It Out (remix) by OutKast

July 02, 2024
blog

The OutKast song 'Walk It Out (remix)' is a catchy and energetic track that invites listeners to groove along and show off their dance moves. The overall theme of the song revolves around self-confidence and embracing one's unique style and swagger. Through a series of standout lyrics, the song explores various themes that contribute to this overarching message.One notable lyric in the song is "If you say real talk, I probably won't trust ya." This line speaks to the idea of authenticity and the skepticism that comes with it. The theme here suggests that true self-confidence stems from being true to oneself and not getting caught up in the facade that others might present. Embracing one's individuality and expressing it honestly is an important aspect of walking it out.Another powerful lyric is "Your white tee, well to me, look like a nightgown. Make your Momma proud, take that thing two sizes down." This lyric delves into the theme of self-image and societal standards. It challenges the notion of conforming to societal expectations and encourages embracing one's unique style. It suggests that true confidence comes from feeling comfortable in one's own skin, regardless of societal expectations.The line "I walked it out the bank, with a lot of zeros. That's what my teachers called me, predictions like they Cleo" introduces the theme of success and overcoming obstacles. It highlights the journey of achieving success and how it can be a form of empowerment. The reference to teachers and predictions alludes to the doubters and naysayers that one may encounter along the way. Walking it out can be seen as a metaphor for pushing through obstacles and proving others wrong.Jim Jones' verse brings forth the theme of regional pride and loyalty. He raps, "Now come holla at me, you know how we get it out here. Eastside walk it out, Bronx walk it out, Queens walk it out, you know Brooklyn walk it out. Now Harlem walk it out, Harlem walk it out." This verse celebrates the diverse neighborhoods and communities that make up New York City, emphasizing the importance of embracing and representing one's roots. It suggests that walking it out is not just about individual confidence but also about showcasing the pride and identity of a community.Finally, Big Boi's verse delves into the theme of creativity and innovation. He raps, "I walk it out like that last shot of 'gnac at the club. Cause the package store was closed and I'm attached to my buzz." This lyric celebrates thinking outside the box and finding unique ways to express oneself. It highlights the ability to adapt and find unconventional solutions when faced with limitations. Walking it out becomes a symbol of breaking free from the norm and embracing one's own creative instincts.In conclusion, 'Walk It Out (remix)' by OutKast is more than just a catchy dance track. It explores a range of themes that encourage self-confidence, individuality, and embracing one's roots. Each standout lyric in the song contributes to the overall theme by adding depth and imagination. Whether it's embracing authenticity, defying societal expectations, celebrating success, representing one's community, or fostering creativity, the message is clear – walking it out means owning who you are and strutting your stuff with confidence and pride. So, put on your dancing shoes, embrace your unique style, and walk it out with pride!
