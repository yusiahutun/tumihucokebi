Parkway Drive drummer Ben Gordon lists his Byron Bay home for $10m

July 05, 2024
blog

Ben Gordon, the drummer for Australian heavy metal band Parkway Drive, has listed his Byron Bay mansion for sale.

The 36-year-old musician hopes to get more than $10million for the four-bedroom, four-bathroom beachside property, which boasts stunning ocean views.

The newly renovated two-storey home has an open-plan design and is a marvel of modern architecture.

It includes an office, gym, central atrium and fireplace, as well as many other features that combine contemporary convenience with traditional elegance.

The interior designer Mel Gubbin told Realestate.com.au the property was perfect for all kinds of lifestyles.

'You can flow from inside to out entertaining or move peacefully from wellness zones on a quiet morning to a relaxing private nook to recharge,' Gubbin said.

Gordon purchased the property for $2.45million in 2019 and has spent the past three years significantly renovating it and increasing its market value.

In 2021, Gordon sold a documentary about his metalcore band to Netflix, despite being a vocal critic of the streaming platform's controversial 'docu-soap' Byron Baes.

While the irony might seem surprising, the drummer - who also owns the Byron Bay General Store café - clarified his issue was not with Netflix itself but simply the concept of the reality show and its potential impact on the Byron Bay community.

He told The Daily Telegraph: 'My issue with Byron Baes was never about Netflix, it was about the content of the show and its potentially damaging effects to our home town.

'Byron has been bombarded with external pressures over the last several years and the announcement of Byron Baes was the straw that broke the camel's back.'

While he acknowledged the show had caused problems for the town, he also praised Netflix for buying the rights to the Parkway Drive documentary.

'The fact that Netflix will be streaming Viva the Underdogs is commendable as it is a genuine Australian story that embodies hard work, perseverance, self belief and friendship, which I think a lot of Australians will relate to,' he said.

Viva the Underdogs follows the Byron Bay band's preparations for headlining the German music festival Wacken Open Air in 2019.
