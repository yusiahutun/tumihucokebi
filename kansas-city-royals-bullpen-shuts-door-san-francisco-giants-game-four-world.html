Kansas City Royals bullpen shuts door on San Francisco Giants in game three of World Series

January 30, 2024
blog

Starter Jeremy Guthrie pitched five shutout innings and the bullpen slammed the door shut as Kansas City Royals held on to beat the San Francisco Giants 3-2 and grab control of the World Series on Friday.

Unbeaten through the post-season until they lost the first game of the World Series at home on Tuesday, the Royals have produced back-to-back victories to take a 2-1 lead in the best-of-seven series.

Alex Gordon, Lorenzo Cain and Eric Hosmer all drove in a run for the Royals while closer Greg Holland came on in the ninth to collect his seventh save of the post-season.

Game four is set for AT&T Park on Saturday.

“We’ve got a bunch of guys in here battling,” said Hosmer. “We’re getting key hits in key situations. We just show no fear.

“We had our back against the wall, season on the line [in wild-card game]. We continue to battle.

“We know we have a dangerous team when post-season baseball comes.

“We’re just trying to get leads and hand off to our bullpen. This is a huge win for us and hats off to the bullpen for holding it down for us.”

The Giants found themselves in an early hole with 39-year-old starter Tim Hudson, the third-oldest pitcher to make a debut start in the World Series, received a rude welcome when the Royals’ Alcides Escobar took his first pitch and lined a double to left then scored on Lorenzo Cain’s ground out.

Hudson settled down after the rocky start and retired 12 successive batters until Escobar collected his second hit of the game with a single in the sixth.

That was quickly followed by an RBI double from Gordon bumping the Royals lead to 2-0.

With two out and a man on second San Francisco manager Bruce Bochy replaced Hudson with Javier Lopez but the Royals scored one more run when Eric Hosmer drove in Gordon.

The Giants answered right back in the bottom of the inning with two runs, Michael Morse sparking the rally with an RBI double and Buster Posey driving in another run to slash the Kansas City lead to 3-2.

The Royals’ bullpen, however, would not crack with Kelvin Herrera, Brandon Finnegan, Wade Davis coming on and pitching shutout relief.

Holland then clinched the victory by retiring the heart of the San Francisco batting order Hunter Pence, Pablo Sandoval and Buster Posey to record the save.

“I wasn’t going to take any chances,” said Royals manager Ned Yost. “It was a pivotal game in my mind.

“I was going to go with my bullpen, Kelvin in the sixth, mix and match the seventh and get to Wade in the eighth and that’s how it worked out.”
