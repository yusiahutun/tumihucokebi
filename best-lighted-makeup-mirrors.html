What to Keep in Mind

Lighting

When you're shopping for a lighted makeup mirror, there are a few specific things to look out for if you want a high-quality option. "Important features are dimmable function for brightness and a function to change color temperature from cool to warm orange hues," says Kinjo, whose celebrity clients include Mindy Kaling, Mary J. Blige, and Uzo Aduba. Our Best Overall pick, the Riki Skinny Smart Vanity Mirror, has five brightness levels to light up all areas of the face.

"For deeper skin tones, a cool white light is horrible lighting and makes skin tones look overly light and ashy," she explains. "Therefore, it is important to have some orangey tungsten hues to warm up the white light. A lighted mirror where you can control the color warmth and get the ideal combo of cool and warm lighting is the right lighted mirror for all complexions."

Even if your bathroom or vanity area is already bright, you might still benefit from a makeup mirror with lights. "[A lighted makeup mirror] allows you to see yourself clearly when applying makeup without the cast of shadows typically caused by overhead lighting," says celebrity makeup artist Steve Kassajikian. "This way you always get an even application and blend."

Magnification

Keep in mind that a typical mirror is 1x, so when perusing makeup mirror options, consider how close up you want it to be. Most makeup mirrors come with some level of magnification like 3x or 5x, which make an object appear 3 times or 5 times larger, to help amplify harder to see areas. If you'll be using your mirror to do some detailed work like plucking hairs or putting on false lashes, look for higher magnification like 10x.

Extra Features

While your initial desire for this product may just be a lighted mirror to get ready in front of, the bonus details will seal the deal. Many mirrors come with extra features like USB ports for charging your phone, speakers for playing music, or smart features (like the streaming feature in the HiMirror Mini) that make for the ultimate primping station. If you know you'll be taking your mirror on trips, look for designs that are portable enough to travel with and can be mounted in different locations.

Your Questions, Answered

Are LED lights best for applying makeup?

The short answer is yes. "LED lights are best because [they] will give you a more natural reflection without washing you out," says Kassajikian. Many lighted makeup mirrors on the market use LED lights, but you'll occasionally find some models with fluorescent and incandescent bulbs. LED lights are also more energy-efficient than other types of lighting, according to the U.S. Department of Energy. Plus, they might actually keep any makeup that's nearby safe, too. "LED bulbs don't emit heat and cool to the touch, so makeup placed by the mirror won't melt," Kinjo says.
