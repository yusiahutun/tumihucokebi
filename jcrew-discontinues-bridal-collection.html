J.Crew Is Discontinuing Its Bridal Collection As OfNow.

July 10, 2024
blog

J.Crew, a trailblazer in the world of bridal e-commerce, announced today that it will be discontinuing its bridal collection to focus more on party dresses, Racked reports. With this little piece of news, fashion-conscious brides with dress budgets under $2,000, short engagements and civil ceremonies have had their bridal fashion hearts crushed. And bridezillas are not the type of consumer worth upsetting–or are they?

J.Crew

After more than a decade in the bridal business, J.Crew's reps confirmed that the brand entered the space–now flooded with competitors like H&M, Ann Taylor, and more–for a piece of the wedding industry pie (which IBISWorld has tallied as worth $3 billion). Initially, they entered the space after CEO Mickey Drexler heard about the bridesmaids market–The New Yorker reported in 2012 that he was interested in hearing more about the business that had brides buying multiples of the same dress in the same color for their friends. According to Racked, what the brand discovered after thousands of gowns, reception dresses, bridal separates, jumpsuits, accessories and bridesmaids dresses was that the production and retail of such a niche business proves taxing, and "financially inefficient." No surprise there: materials like tulle, beading, silk and lace are costly–and the consumer on the hunt for them is more demanding than even those after the priciest of the brand's Collection dresses, which at times retail for more than one of its bridal pieces. Rather than cut costs on production and quality, a move that stands in complete contrast to its core values, J.Crew opted to cut the collection altogether instead.

J.Crew

All of the bridal styles on the site have now been slashed to 30 percent markdowns, and none of the items listed will be replenished. Now, the brand will focus on its party and occasion dresses, expanding that range to continue to please the droves of bridesmaids flooding the site. But now, more importantly, where is the cost and time efficient bride to shop? Many chic retailers have followed suit in the 10 years J.Crew has been in the space, so fret not. Reformation, FWRD, BHLDN, Net-a-Porter and most recently, Whistles, have all jumped on the bridal e-tail train with options to suit a variety of different styles, budgets and sizes.
