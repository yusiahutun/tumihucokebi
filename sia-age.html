Sia began her musical career in the mid-1990s as a vocalist with a local Adelaide acid jazz band, Crisp. She provided vocals to two of its songs, Word and the Deal, released in 1996, and Delirium in1997.

Crisp disbanded in 1997, and on December 23of the same year, Sia released her debut studio album, OnlySee. It consisted of 13 tracks and sold 1200

Then Sia relocated to London, where she got involved with the British funk and acid jazz band Jamiroquaias a backup vocalist.

Sia eventually became an unofficial lead singer for the downtempo group and traveled with them. The singer also provided vocals to their first three studio albums.

Her vocal contributions to Zero 7 include the tunes Destiny and Distractions from the first album, Simple Things, Somersault, and Speed Dial No.2. Also, from the second album, When It Falls, and five tracks from the third album, The Garden

Meanwhile, after obtaining a deal with Dance Pool, a sub-label of Sony Music, she released her debut song, Taken for Granted, in 2000. The song was ranked at No. 10 on the UK Singles Chart.
