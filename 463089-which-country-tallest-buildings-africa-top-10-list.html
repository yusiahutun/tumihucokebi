Which country has the tallest buildings in Africa? Top 10 list

The African skyline has been changing over the years. Cities are expanding, lives are changing, and the economy in Africa is also witnessing a gradual transformation. Which country has the tallest building in Africa? South Africa has had the tallest building in Africa, the Carlton Centre, which was the highest tower in the African skyline from its completion in 1973 until 2019. Post-2019 other countries have had taller towers than those sitting in South Africa. So, which country has the tallest buildings in Africa?

Every capital city in Africa is growing thanks to the spirit of free enterprise and competitive markets across the globe. African and foreign investors are financing modern and sophisticated buildings every year.

Many countries are building commercial hubs and even strive to have lofty buildings because of the gains they fetch. Which country has the tallest building? South Africa may not have the tallest building on the continent, but it ranks as the country with the most elevated towers in Africa.

Which country has the tallest buildings in Africa?

Africa is not generally known for its abundance of skyscrapers. However, it has undergone a building surge in different cities. As a result, the number of tall buildings has grown significantly throughout the continent over the last decade. The following are the tallest buildings according to ConstructionKenya:

1. The Iconic Tower, Egypt

The construction of this magnificent building started in 2018, and it was completed in August 2021. The Iconic Tower is not only a sight to behold but also the tallest building in Africa.

This giant tower is the New Administrative Tower in the North African country. It has 80 floors and stands at 1266 feet or 385 meters. The tower covers an area of 7,100,000 square meters.

2. Mohammed VI Tower, Rabat, Morocco

Mohammed VI tower has an eye-catching shape. The building resembles a rocket on its launch pad. Standing at 820 feet high, the Mohammed VI Tower is the second tallest building in Africa.

This building is tall enough to be visible from a distance of about 50 kilometres. The tower was completed in 2022 and has an area of 102,800 square meters.

3. The Leonardo Sandton, South Africa

Standing at 768 feet or 234 meters high, The Leonardo is another of the stunning towering buildings in Africa. It has 55 floors, with most of its spaces used for offices and others for commercial purposes.

The Leonardo was constructed between 2015 and 2019. This is one of the most expensive buildings to rent in South Africa, whether you are looking for an office or an apartment to live in.

4. Carlton Centre Johannesburg, South Africa

South Africa is not just a powerhouse in Africa, but it is also one of the African countries with the tallest buildings that are also fancy and stylish. Carton Centre is one of the striking buildings in the country’s capital and one of the tallest in South Africa.

Carlton Centre stands at 731 feet or 223 meters tall. The Carlton Centre has been the tallest building in Africa for about 40 years since its completion in 1973. At the Carlton Centre, you can rent office space, shops, and apartments to live in.

5. Commercial Bank of Ethiopia Tower Addis Ababa, Ethiopia

CBE Tower is the headquarters of the Commercial Bank of Ethiopia. Standing at 685 feet tall or 209 meters high, the tower is the tallest building in East Africa. This Ethiopian skyscraper sits on 165476 square meters and was completed in February 2022. CBE Tower has office spaces, cafeterias, a shopping mall, exhibition areas, and entertainment joints.

6. Britam Tower, Kenya

Britam Tower’s prismatic shape stands out on the Nairobi skyline. This distinctive building has 32 floors and stands 640 feet tall (195 meters). Britam Tower’s unique shape is of an equal four-sided base which develops into a 60-meter mass two-sided roof with three helical wind turbines.

Britam Tower is the headquarter of the Britam Holdings financial services company. The building also contains office spaces and rooms for other commercial activities.

7. Global Trade Centre Tower, Nairobi, Kenya

The GTC Tower is the latest entrant in the Nairobi skyline. It is striking and visible from various locations in the city. The building, which is 184 meters tall, has six imposing towers.

Construction of the Global Trade Center Tower started in 2015, and the completion was done in 2021. The building consists of a mall, offices, apartments, restaurants, and other commercial hubs. GTC Tower has 43 floors.

8. Ponte City, Johannesburg, South Africa

Ponte City is 173 meters tall (567.6 feet). The skyscraper was built in 1975 and has for the longest time been one of the tallest buildings in South Africa’s capital. Its cylindrical shape is iconic, and its location gives one a 360-degree view of Johannesburg.

The centre space of this glorious building is known as ‘The Core’ as it rises above an uneven rock floor. Ponte City has 55 floors and is the tallest residential skyscraper in the Southern hemisphere.

9. UAP Old Mutual Tower, Nairobi, Kenya

The 33-storey building is one of the tallest in Kenya’s capital.UAP Tower was completed in 2015 and was then the tallest building in Kenya, surpassing Times Tower, which had been the tallest for 15 years.

This building is 163 meters (535 feet) tall and mostly consists of office spaces and retail shops. UAP Tower is owned by Old Mutual Holdings.

10. NECOM House, Lagos, Nigeria

NECOM House has been having different names since its construction. It was formerly known as NITEL Tower and, before that, the NET Building. This skyscraper stands 160 meters (525 feet) tall.

NECOM House was the tallest building in West Africa at the time of its completion in 1979. It houses the headquarters of NITEL, a Nigerian telecommunications company, and is also a commercial hub.
