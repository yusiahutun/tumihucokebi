In his Seventies heyday he was famed for a lion's mane of hair, spectacular flares and a willingness to display his chest.

Now only the open-necked shirt remains.

Barry Gibb, the 62-year-old Bee Gee, looked a far cry from the image we all remember as he waited to go through security at Los Angeles airport.

The white figure-hugging silk outfits have given way to dark trousers and a baggy grey hooded sweatshirt.

Barry Gibb queues at a security checkpoint at LA airport

A baseball cap hides the greying and less bouffant hair.

Accompanied by his wife Linda and daughter Alexandra, the father-of-five queued inside the terminal with other passengers as he prepared to board his flight.

The family were understood to be returning from Australia, where Gibb had joined a host of music stars for the Sound Relief concerts.

On Saturday, he duetted with Olivia Newton John to close the event in Sydney.

The concerts, which took place in Melbourne as well as Sydney, raised money for victims of the bushfires which ravaged Australia last month.

'I think our spirits are always here,' said Mr Gibb, who was raised in Australia but now has homes in Miami and Buckinghamshire.

At least 210 people were killed and more than 2,000 homes destroyed as the fires swept through southern Victoria.

Barry's brother Maurice died in January 2003. He and Robin initially vowed to continue as the Bee Gees, but later changed their minds.
