FAQs

Q: What is Aperol Spritz?

A: Aperol Spritz is a popular Italian cocktail made with Aperol, Prosecco, and soda water. It is typically served over ice and garnished with a slice of orange.

Q: Where did Aperol Spritz originate?

A: Aperol Spritz originated in Italy, specifically in the Veneto region. It was first created in the early 1900s but gained significant popularity in the 1950s.

Q: What does Aperol Spritz taste like?

A: Aperol Spritz has a bittersweet taste with notes of orange and herbs. It is a refreshing and well-balanced cocktail that is both sweet and tangy.

Q: Can I substitute Aperol with another ingredient?

A: While Aperol is the traditional choice for making an Aperol Spritz, you can experiment with other aperitivo liqueurs such as Campari or Cynar to create a different flavor profile.

Q: Is Aperol Spritz a low-alcohol cocktail?

A: Yes, Aperol Spritz is considered a low-alcohol cocktail. Aperol has a lower alcohol content compared to other spirits, making it a lighter option for those who prefer a moderate alcohol intake.

Q: How do I make an Aperol Spritz?

A: To make an Aperol Spritz, combine 3 parts Prosecco, 2 parts Aperol, and 1 part soda water in a glass filled with ice. Stir gently and garnish with an orange slice.
