Sanaipei Tande's education

She attended Loreto Convent Mombasa for her primary school education. She enrolled at Mama Ngina High School for her high school education before transferring to Agha Khan High School while still living with her parents in Mombasa. After relocating to Nairobi, she continued her high school studies at St Lucy Kiriri Girls.

Upon completing her high school studies, she was admitted to Kenya Medical Training College to study Pharmacy. However, she didn't complete her studies as she opted to defer and temporarily pursue a career in music, which was her passion.

Career

Upon winning 2004 East Africa Coca Cola Popstars Talent Search, Sana, and the other band members, namely, Pam Waithaka and Kevin Kev Waweru, released their debut single dubbed Leta Wimbo which was followed by seventeen other track albums.

Unfortunately, the band dismantled in 2005, after which the singer started her solo career. She became a presenter at Capital FM and worked alongside Eve D'Souza on the show Hits, not Homework.

In 2006, Tande joined Kiss FM before moving on to Easy FM as the presenter of the mid-morning drive show together with Oga Obinna and Edward Kwach.

Sanaipei Tande also began hosting karaoke in different clubs and bar across Nairobi, and she is still doing the same to date. Apart from radio and music, Sanaipei has featured in several local shows where she has played top roles such as Kina and Aziza.
