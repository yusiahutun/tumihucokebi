Mom-of-three admits she feels like she has 'lost herself' and 'wasted her education'

March 28, 2024
blog

A mother-of-three has penned an emotional message about how being a dedicated parent to her children has left her feeling like she 'lost herself' and 'wasted her education.'  

Kate Swenson, 36, from Minnesota, has been writing about motherhood on her blog Finding Cooper's Voice since her eldest son, Cooper, was diagnosed with severe, non-verbal autism. 

The working mom, who also has sons Sawyers and Harbor, took to Facebook on February 3 to open up about how all the sacrifices she has made as a parent has turned her into a shadow of her former self.

Candid image: Kate shared a photo of herself hunched over on a stool while trying to potty train two of her sons as part of her lengthy post 

As part of her post, she shared a black and white photo of herself hunched over on a stool while trying to potty train her two youngest boys.   

'I’m having a serious case of lost identity. Or maybe it’s a midlife crisis. Or a little post-partum depression. Or maybe I’m just tired, overweight and mentally drained. Who knows which one,' she wrote. 

'I have three boys, a husband, a home, and a job I love. I am beyond blessed. I have devoted my life to the humans in my life.'

Kate stressed that on 'most days' she is usually happy to take care of her family, but she doesn't always feel that way.    

'I feel like I have lost myself along the way,' she admitted. 'I look in the mirror and I hardly recognize myself anymore. And as I approach middle agedness, that is really scary. 

'It’s not that I necessary look old. It’s that I look like someone I don’t even recognize. I look tired. I look like I’ve let myself go. I look angry. I look really rushed.'

Kate said she is always rushing to shower, eat, pick up or drop off her kids, and get dinner on the table. 

She noted that she has always been the one who can 'handle anything' and is 'typically unrattled,' but lately, she feels 'almost empty.'   

'When I’m with my kids I feel guilty for not working and when I’m working I feel guilty for not playing with my kids more. It’s a lose-lose at times,' she explained. 

'I feel like I wasted my education. I feel like a housekeeper, a cook, a [chauffeur], and a ring leader. I feel like I always have sick kids and I can't finish the laundry or squeeze my butt into my fat jeans. 

'I know I’m a good mom. I don’t doubt myself in that department,' she added. 'But I also feel like all I am is a mom sometimes.'

The mom said she finds herself getting upset by little things. She has even been jealous of her husband, Jamie, getting to go to the bathroom alone.

She noted that just the other day they drew straws to determine who would go upstairs and change the sheets after one of their sons wet his bed.   

'I didn’t know that I could consider a shower a luxury either. Or not have time to do it. It blows my mind,' she continued. 'I am a very capable woman and I can’t find time to shower. I laugh even as I type it.'  

Kate said she is also 'too tired to care' about how she looks, something she used to take great pride in. 

'I used to care about how I looked. Like really care. I ate well. I exercised. I showered. I put makeup on. I’d peek at hashtags on Instagram like "outfits for summer" and pin cute outfits. Now, I wear hoodies. Dirty ones. Grey T-shirts. I grab my clothes off of the floor every morning.'

She stressed that she knows 'there are so many more important things to do than look cute,' and she is happy to focus on sleep, her children, and work.   

'But then, I see myself, and feel sad. I feel like I’ve lost myself. Almost entirely,' she explained. 'I have no hobbies. I have no time to do anything. I just care for kids. For my home. I keep the ship going.

Plan: She admitted that she doesn't know how to 'fix this funk,' but she is working on it because she doesn't want to miss this time in her sons' lives or resent it

'I watch TV shows in 15 minute increments. I stare at my phone for entertainment. I answer text messages three days late. I’m nonstop busy and yet bored at the same time. It’s a bizarre way to feel.'

Kate said she doesn't know how to 'fix this funk,' but she is working on it because she doesn't want to miss this time in her sons' lives or resent it.   

'I don’t want to be angry,' she said. 'What I’ve learned is that there is only so much of me. And I need to find balance.

'This year I will find balance in motherhood, marriage, my job, my home and my sanity. That's my goal. Learn to laugh more too and give myself more grace.'

Kate's candid post has been shared more than 5,000 times and received thousands of comments.  

'Oh Kate reading this makes my heart hurt for you. I don’t have advice to "fix" it but I can tell you that you will figure it out,' one woman wrote. 'You’re stronger than you know. We as moms all have been in this season and you will find your way out of it. Sending you lots of hugs and prayers.'  

'I’m in a dark, dark place today. There’s so much screaming. I’m so tired. I just want today to be over. I desperately need a time out. I desperately need a minute without someone demanding something of me,' another added. 'I see you. I hear you. I’m sorry. Strength be with you.'
