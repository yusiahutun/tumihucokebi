Early Life

Kathy Griffin was born on November 4, 1960, in Oak Park, Illinois. She grew up in a Catholic family and has four older siblings.

Stand-Up Comedy Career

Kathy started her career in the 1980s as a stand-up comedian performing in various comedy clubs across the United States.

Reality Television

Kathy gained widespread recognition for her reality television show titled “Kathy Griffin: My Life on the D-List” which aired from 2005 to 2010.

Grammy Award-Winning Album

She released a comedy album called “Kathy Griffin: Calm Down Gurrl” in 2013, which won the Grammy Award for Best Comedy Album.

Bravo TV Specials

Kathy has hosted several stand-up comedy specials on Bravo TV, including “Kathy Griffin: My Life on the Bravo Stage” and “Kathy Griffin: Gurrl Down!”

Acting Career

In addition to her comedy work, Kathy has appeared in various television shows and films such as “Seinfeld,” “Pulp Fiction,” and “Suddenly Susan.”

Controversial Comedy

Kathy is known for her bold and controversial comedic style, often making jokes about celebrities and public figures which have landed her in hot water.

Emmy Award-Winning Reality Show

Her reality show “Kathy Griffin: My Life on the D-List” won two Emmy Awards in 2007 and 2008 for Outstanding Reality Program.

New York Times Bestselling Author

Kathy has written several books, including “Official Book Club Selection: A Memoir According to Kathy Griffin” which became a New York Times bestseller.

LGBTQ+ Advocate

Griffin has been a vocal supporter of LGBTQ+ rights and has used her platform to raise awareness and funds for various LGBTQ+ organizations.

Guinness World Record

In 2013, Kathy Griffin earned a Guinness World Record for having the most stand-up specials by a comedian.

Comedy Tour

Kathy has embarked on several successful comedy tours, entertaining audiences across the United States with her sharp wit and unfiltered humor.

Talk Show Host

In 2012, Kathy hosted her own talk show on Bravo TV called “Kathy.” The show featured celebrity interviews and comedic segments.

Social Media Presence

Griffin is active on various social media platforms and has a large following. She often shares her thoughts, comedic sketches, and behind-the-scenes moments.

Honorary Gay Men

Griffin is known for referring to her gay male friends as her “honorary gay men” and has developed a strong connection within the LGBTQ+ community.

Political Activism

Kathy has been an outspoken critic of certain politicians and has actively supported and campaigned for progressive causes.

Multiple Awards and Nominations

Throughout her career, Griffin has received numerous awards and nominations, including the GLAAD Vanguard Award and a star on the Hollywood Walk of Fame.

Fashion Police Co-Host

From 2015 to 2016, Kathy served as a co-host on the television show “Fashion Police,” offering her humorous commentary on celebrity fashion.

Stand-Up Special: “Kathy Griffin: A Hell of a Story”

In 2019, Griffin released a stand-up comedy special titled “Kathy Griffin: A Hell of a Story” where she reflected on her experiences following a controversial photo scandal.

International Performances

Kathy has taken her comedy tours internationally, performing in countries such as the United Kingdom, Canada, and Australia.

Theater Performances

Griffin has also showcased her talents on the theatrical stage, starring in productions like “The Vagina Monologues” and “Laugh Your Head Off.”

Personal Fitness Journey

Kathy has been open about her weight loss journey and fitness regimen, often including references to her exercise routines in her comedic performances.

Wedding Ceremonies

Kathy has been married twice. Her first marriage was to Matt Moline from 2001 to 2006, and her second marriage is to Randy Bick since 2020.

Broad Comedy Style

Kathy’s comedic style encompasses a wide range of topics, including pop culture, politics, religion, and personal experiences, ensuring there’s something for everyone.

Voice Acting

Unique Fashion Choices

Docu-Comedy Series

Entertaining Live Audiences
