Green Card Lottery: Over 3k Lucky Kenyans Win Chance to Migrate to US

March 31, 2024
blog
  • A total of 22,185,619 individuals applied for the US Green Card 2024 globally, according to the latest data
  • Of the total applicants, only 143,000 will get letters notifying them to apply for an immigrant visa at the various U.S. embassies
  • Kenya is the biggest winner after 3760 Kenyans won the opportunity to get permanent USA residency

The United States Department of State has released the latest data on people selected under the Diversity Visa 2024 (Green Card) lottery.

How many people won US Green Card 2024 globally

According to the data, 3,760 Kenyans won the chance to migrate to the USA under the Green Card lottery programme.

Globally, 22,185,619 individuals submitted entries during the DV-2024 registration period from October 5, 2022, to November 8, 2022.

Out of those who applied, only 143,000 will get letters notifying them to apply for an immigrant visa at the various US embassies globally, with Kenya having the highest number of winners globally.

However, despite the many applicants notified, only 55,000 visas are available for this period.

How can one be denied a US permanent residency visa?

With the limited slots available, some winners might be locked out from getting permanent resident visas for several reasons.

In most incidents, applicants may be ineligible based on education requirements or mistakes in their application forms.

Applicants who lied during the application process are typically denied the visas.

Those who won the Green Card lottery must complete an application for permanent residency, undergo a mandatory interview at the US consulate, and be granted permanent residence by September 30, 2024.

The US government recently suspended interviews for Kenyans seeking renewal of travel visas.

In a statement, the US ambassador to Kenya, Meg Whitman, announced that the United States embassy expanded the interview waiver program for nonimmigrant visas.

The consulate explained that the above measure would enable them to reduce the travel visa interview appointment backlog.

"As part of Ambassador Whitman’s pledge to reduce the nonimmigrant visa interview appointment backlog, the United States Embassy in Nairobi announced today that it has expanded the Interview Waiver Program for nonimmigrant visas," the embassy said in a statement.
