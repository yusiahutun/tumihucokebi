Andre discusses dealing with the pressure of being the son of a famous football player.

He is the son of three-time African player of the year and FIFA 100 member Abedi “Pele” Ayew, and both of his uncles were professional footballers. He was born into it as the son of one of the greatest players in Africa.

Growing up as the son of a football star, Andre was subjected to a great deal of pressure.

Andre stated on a podcast that he grew up under pressure, and because he was unable to change the situation, it became a part of him. In addition, they had high expectations of him because of his father, which was difficult.

Even if he worked hard and succeeded, his name would come after his father’s, which was first difficult for him to accept. His principal objective was to establish his reputation. He worked diligently and resolutely, taking criticism in stride.

He stated that having a father who was the greatest African player of all time guarantees that you will be envious, even if you have done nothing to them.

Andre believes he has dealt with all that has transpired in his life. He feels that athletes who cannot handle pressure will not be able to compete at the highest level.

At the age of 12, he became aware of the pressure since his entire family expected him to perform at the highest level and play for the Ghana national team, which was a good thing.

At the same time, he was under a great deal of strain, since everyone was watching him and telling him he had to succeed.

He was questioned at a young age if he could be like his father, if he could play like his father, etc. It became Andre’s responsibility to prove to everyone that he could.
