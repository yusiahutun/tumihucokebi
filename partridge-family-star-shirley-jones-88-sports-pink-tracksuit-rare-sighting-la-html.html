Partridge Family star Shirley Jones, 88, sports a pink tracksuit in a rare sighting in LA

Rarely seen Hollywood veteran Shirley Jones cuts a sporty figure as she steps out for dinner with friends in Los Angeles.

The retired actress dined at Delmonico's Steak and Lobster House, a popular upscale eatery in Encino, last week and stood out in a pink track suit, which she paired with a visor and sneakers. 

The 88-year-old actress and singer found fame in the 1970s sitcom The Partridge Family, where she played the lead role of Shirley Partridge, a widowed mother of five children. 

Her career blossomed from then as she starred in classics, including The Music Man, Carousel, Oklahoma! and Bedtime Story.

Shirley's real family life mirrored the happy family dynamic of her famous TV show as she has 13 grandchildren and three adult sons.

But in 2015, she suffered the loss of her second husband, actor and comedian Marty Ingels, after 38 years of marriage.

Her stepson David Cassidy, who co-starred with her on The Partridge Family, passed away in 2017. 

His father was Shirley's first husband, legendary actor and singer Jack Cassidy, and the pair were married for 18 years. 

Jack died in 1974, two years after he and Shirley divorced.

DailyMail.com can reveal where the rest of the beloved cast of The Partridge Family are now, 50 years on from the show that made them famous. 

SUSAN DEY  

Susan Dey -  Laurie Partridge

After her success on The Partridge Family, Dey, 69, went on to star as Grace Van Owen on the drama L.A. Law from 1986-1992, which won her a Golden Globe for Best Actress in 1988. 

She has kept a low profile since the early 2000s and according to Shirley Jones' memoir, she 'consistently refused' to take part in any Partridge Family reunions after the show ended. 

She had a relationship with co-star David Cassidy, but he claimed in his memoir that he broke it off and she cut ties with him. 

Dey also reportedly suffered from an eating disorder while filming the hit sitcom, which may contribute to her refusal to participate in reunions.  

She was married to talent manager Leonard 'Lenny' Hirshan from 1976 to 1981 and they share a daughter. 

Dey has been married to producer Bernard Sofronski since 1988. 

DAVID CASSIDY 

David Cassidy - Keith Partridge  

David Cassidy passed away in 2017 at the age of 67. 

Before his death, he revealed he had been diagnosed with dementia and was hospitalized for liver and kidney failure. 

He was the lead singer on The Partridge Family and the role launched him into teen idol stardom. 

He began recording his own music that led to a successful solo career and five studio albums. 

Cassidy continued acting throughout his life but struggled with substance abuse and was arrested several times for DUI. 

Cassidy's on-screen mother Shirley Jones was his real life stepmom after she married his father Jack Cassidy.  

DANNY BONADUCE 

Danny Bonaduce - Danny Partridge 

Danny Bonaduce, 62, landed several guest spots after his role as Danny Partridge ended. In 2005 he had his own reality show Breaking Bonaduce where he confronted his battles with drug and alcohol addiction. 

The radio host suffered a health scare earlier this year but is on the mend. He was hit with a mystery illness in May that affected his ability to speak and walk.

But by June, the radio DJ returned to work at KZOK in Seattle, where he co-hosts The Danny Bonaduce & Sarah Morning Show. 

He began treatment in April when his wife, Amy, first noticed his speech was off and called for help immediately. 

While speaking of his condition, he noted that he 'couldn't walk, keep his balance, was slurring really badly and had trouble remembering anything.' 

But in June, he announced, 'Great news. I'll be back live on the radio this Monday. Can't wait. My brain is still a little fuzzy but that's never stopped me — or the people who hire me.'

He has two children, Countess Isabella and Count Dante from his second marriage.

SUZANNE CROUGH  

Suzanne Crough - Tracy Partridge 

Crough played the youngest Partridge sibling Tracy and made few on screen appearances after the show's end. 

Her last acting role came in the 1980 TV movie Children of Divorce, where she played Kate. 

She went on to live a fairly normal life and graduated rom Los Angeles Pierce College, owned a bookstore, married and had two daughters. 

During a Partridge Family reunion in 2010 she said she was the manager of an Office Max in Arizona. 

Crough died suddenly in 2015 at the age of 52 after suffering from a rare form of cardiomyopathy. 

'Everyone thought I'd be the first Partridge to go. Sadly it was little Tracy,' Danny Bonaduce said of Crough's death. 'Suzanne was a wonderful lady and a good mom. She will be missed.'

BRIAN FORSTER 

Brian Forster - Chris Partridge

Brian Forster was not the first to star in the role of Chris Partridge. 

He landed the part after the original drummer Chris Partridge, played by Jeremy Gelbwaks, left the show in 1971. 

He spoke about being cast in the show during an interview, and said, 'I remember being in fourth or fifth grade, dancing around to ''I Think I Love You'' and all of a sudden I was going to be on the show, so I was pretty excited.' 

The 62-year-old has remained lowkey since the end of the show in 1974, though he has appeared at Partridge Family reunions. 

He continued to take on acting roles and even dabbled in race car driving in California in the early 2000s. 

DAVE MADDEN 

Dave Madden - Reuben Kincaid

Madden was best known for his role as Reuben Kincaid, the Partridge's manager.  

Danny Bonaduce said in an interview that he owes a lot to Madden after he took the young actor in while he was going through family issues. 

Madden was a Canadian-American comedian who appeared on the Ed Sullivan Show and had several acting jobs after the end of the sitcom. 

He had a recurring role as the diner customer Earl Hicks on the sitcom Alice until the mid 80s.

Madden died in 2014 at the age of 82 from complications of myelodysplastic syndrome.
