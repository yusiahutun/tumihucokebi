The 7 Best Black Lipsticks, According to Makeup Artists

September 26, 2024
blog

Black lipstick is one of the boldest makeup looks you can rock. It’s dark, moody, and impossible to ignore. And now, the glam-grunge lip look is on the rise again.

“The ‘90s and goth-inspired makeup are coming back, especially with browns and blacks,” says celebrity makeup artist Tobi Henney, noting Gen Z’s embrace of the deeper, darker hues.

Naturally, the first step to pulling off black lipstick is finding the right formula. “A black lipstick has to have great opacity and staying power so it looks good the entire time being worn,” says Henney.

Here, we’ve tapped Henney along with two other pro makeup artists to discover their all-time favorite black lipsticks.

What to Keep in Mind

Formula + Finish

Black can be a tricky shade because it’s so dark. So, formula-wise, you want something with staying power as the contrast between black and your lips is so strong that it will be extremely noticeable if the color fades with time. ”I don’t want something that is going to transfer like crazy or wear off easily, leaving the lips looking patchy,” says Amick. That’s why most of the above lipsticks are liquid matte. This type of formula goes on wet and dries down to really adhere to your lips.

Undertones

Black is made by mixing all the colors. So, depending on how much of what is added, a discerning eye can pick up different undertones in shades of black. “A black lipstick can have various undertones, which gives it that unique wearability,” says Scibelli. “I like to look for black lipsticks with a range of hues like deep blues, plums, and deep reds.”

Your Questions, Answered

Can you layer a matte lipstick with gloss?

Yes! Just be sure to allow the lipstick to fully dry down before going in with gloss. “I love it with a gloss on top,” says Henney, recommending MAC Lipglass ($23) in Clear.

I’m hesitant about wearing black lipstick — what should I do?

“You need to be confident and excited to pull off any bold look, including a black lipstick,” says Henney. So, if you’re feeling inspired and ready to make a statement, go for it! But if you’re feeling really uneasy, Scibelli says you can work up to black by starting with deeper plums and berry tones.
