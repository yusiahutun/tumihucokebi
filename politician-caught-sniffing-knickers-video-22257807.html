Politician caught sniffing knickers in video council meeting claims it's a 'joke'

This is the bizarre moment a Brazilian politician sniffs a pair of women’s knickers while in an online council meeting – mistakenly thinking the camera is off.

The politician, who is a member of a Christian party, said he had the undies sent him in the post by a mischievous pal.

In the video, politicians discuss a planning issue when council member Ditinho do Asilo, of the Social Christian Party, pulls out a pair of small red knickers.

He studies the pants in depth before lifting the gusset to his nose and taking a sniff – in what he claims was a "joke".

Trying to explain his strange actions, he says he had received a present in the mail during the session and deactivated his microphone when he collected it.

He said he was then checking to see what he had been given, thinking that his video was deactivated along with his microphone.

The bizarre incident occurred during an online meeting of the Braganca Paulista Town Council, a town in the southeastern Brazilian state of Sao Paulo, where they were discussing chopping down trees in the area.

Asilo said: “It was a misfortune of my lack of practice with this technology.

"I wore a pink shirt and, as a joke, a friend sent me these knickers.

"It was an edible item from one of those sex shops. That’s why I sniffed it. I have a family.

“It was just a joke that, because of my naivety, became public.”

He added that he did not mean to disrespect fellow councillor Fabiana Alessandri, adding: “I agree that it wasn’t the moment to do this, but I wasn’t speaking, I was following the agenda.

“When a councillor is at home, they can be doing anything when they join a session and I went to open the package and I was caught.”

The council are looking into the incident and have not yet decided if any measures will be taken, according to reports.
