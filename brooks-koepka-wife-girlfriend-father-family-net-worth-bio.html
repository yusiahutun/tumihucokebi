Brooks Koepka Biography

Brooks Koepka was born on 3 May 1990 in West Palm Beach, Florida. He grew up in a family that was sports-mad, and this will be one of the first things that will drive him and his brother Chase Koepka to play golf. Surprisingly, their father Bob Koepka played baseball, and the boys loved the sport too when they grew up.

For his education, the man who would grow up to be a great golfer went to Cardinal Newman High School, and from there he went to Florida State University.

What got him into the spotlight and kept him there is his career as a golfer. He started out as an amateur golfer who continued to play in his school days. By the time he was 13 years old, Brooks had already taken the sport very seriously and he never stopped.

Brooks had his first PGA tour in 2014, which he finished in third place before finishing fourth at the U.S. Open, giving him the chance to play in his first Masters.

During his golfing years, he has managed to stand out from the crowd, and this has helped him to reach a top 7 position in the world rankings in 2017. In 2018 he was between 8th and 11th place, and in his amateur years, he won the Rice Planters Amateur in 2009, the Brickyard Collegiate in 2011, and the Seminole Intercollegiate in Florida in 2012.

He has also won 4 Challenge Tours, 2 European Tours, 2 PGA Tours, and several Japan Golf Tours. He has also been awarded the Sir Henry Cotton Rookie of the Year 2014.
