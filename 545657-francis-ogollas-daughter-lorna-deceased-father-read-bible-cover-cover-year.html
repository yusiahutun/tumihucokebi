Francis Ogolla's Daughter Lorna Says Deceased Father Read Bible Cover to Cover Every Year

  • While making a speech during the burial, she disclosed that the deceased soldier was fond of reading the holy book from cover to cover
  • She added that during difficult years, like in 2022, Ogolla would read the whole Bible twice in the same year

In a heartfelt tribute to her late father, Lorna Ogolla shared a remarkable testament to his unwavering faith and dedication.

General Francis Ogolla may have been a Chief of the Defence Forces, but his love for God remained resolute.

Lorna Ogolla recalls father reading Bible during difficult days

While addressing mourners during her father's funeral, Lorna praised the military man's devout spiritual practice of reading the Bible.

Lorna disclosed that her father was always close to his Bible and read it from cover to cover every year.

She said the tradition brought him immense peace and understanding, especially during challenging moments.

"I remember in 2022, which was an extremely difficult period for him, he read it cover to cover twice in the same year," she said.

Francis Ogolla returned to university in adulthood

The same spirit was embodied in the man's love for working hard, with the daughter indicating that he never had to force them to perform.

Arguably, one of the family's most memorable moments was their father's return to the university to study with his children's peers.

The decision was difficult as he had to commute between Nanyuki and the University of Nairobi.

"Operation Linda Nchi was in progress, and he had a lot of responsibilities, but this man excelled with first-class honours," she said.

My father worked for God, Lorna

Lorna posited that her father worked as if he did it for God, his primary audience, and they saw it.

"Because we saw him do it, we were inspired to do it, and that's the easiest way to teach your children," she praised.

Based on her father's example, Lorna advised parents to lead by example instead of telling their children what to do.

She also advised the public to pursue excellence and work as if no one else was watching because the reward was in Heaven.

"My father didn't need the title to lead, he had so many ideas and a big heart for this nation. He had a big love for his family," Lorna reiterated.

Lorna's sentiments corroborated her brother Joel's speech in which he indicated that their father never left the good book behind.

He added that his father's commitment to this annual ritual was more than just reading but a way of life he embraced wholeheartedly.

"I am sure that his Bible is lying in that wreckage. If they manage to find it, I would like to keep it as a momento," he requested.

His family disclosed this practice was a source of strength and guidance for him, influencing his actions and decisions throughout his life.
