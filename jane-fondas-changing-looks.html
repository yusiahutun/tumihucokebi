Jane Fonda's Beauty Evolution From the '50s to Today

February 20, 2024
blog

Award-winning actress, activist, and model, Jane Fonda, needs no introduction (okay, maybe for Gen Z). With a career spanning more than half a century, she has delighted us with infamous workout videos (which have made a comeback, by the way), inspired us with her speeches, and even managed to give us shoe envy with the most controversial of footwear choices. Now in her 80s, Fonda shows no signs of slowing down and continues to handle whatever comes her way with style and Grace (wink, wink). To celebrate the all-around icon, we take a look at her best beauty moments across her impressive career.

Keep scrolling for Jane Fonda's beauty evolution from the '50s to today.

1955

The fresh-faced Vassar student used her modeling wages to fund classes at the legendary Actors Studio.

1959

Bouncy waves and major makeup for the up-and-coming actress! Fonda described her first Warner Brothers studio makeover in her autobiography, My Life So Far: "I was horrified, dumbstruck. My lips had a new shape and my eyebrows were dark and enormous, like eagle's wings."

1960

Fonda was stunning in her screen debut, Tall Story, but the behind-the-scenes feedback wasn't as encouraging: "[The studio rep] suggested that after filming I might consider having my jaw broken and reset and my back teeth pulled to create a more chiseled look."

1962

She went brunette to play the seductive Kitty Twist in Walk on the Wild Side and earned a Golden Globe for the role.

1963

After filming Sunday in New York, Fonda was introduced to director (and future husband) Roger Vadim in Paris, leading to a Bardot-inspired new look.

1963

Wigging out! Fonda sported a black flip for In the Cool of the Day.

1966

Living in France as a strawberry blonde, Fonda exuded '60s mod vibes.

1967

The Barefoot in the Park star wore her glossy mane pulled into a low ponytail and prepped to play the ultra-sexy titular character in Barbarella.

1971

An iconic new look (and soon an Oscar)! "I had my first real hair epiphany," Fonda wrote. "The men in my life liked it long and blond, and I had been a blonde for so long that I didn't even know what my own color actually was. I simply said to [ hairstylist] Paul McGregor, 'Do something,' and he did. It was the haircut that became famous in Klute, the shag, and he dyed my hair darker, like what it really was. I knew right away that I could do life differently with this hair."

1976

The activist and mother of two (now married to politician Tom Hayden) made The Blue Bird and wore her brunette strands in long, glossy waves complete with Birkin bangs.

1979

Fonda sent off the '70s with voluminous loose waves and sultry eyes for the China Syndrome and a second Oscar for her role in Coming Home.

1982

A busy beauty: In the early '80s Fonda produced 9 to 5, On Golden Pond and her trailblazing Workout video.

1984

After winning an Emmy playing a weather-beaten Appalachian folk artist in The Dollmaker, she rocked big hair and pure '80s glam for a publicity shot.

1990

Divorced from her second husband and dating media mogul Ted Turner, Fonda worked a voluminous blowout at the Academy Awards.

1993

The veteran actress wore a luxuriant waist-length faux braid and wispy bangs to celebrate the Fonda family's induction into the Hollywood Entertainment Museum.

1993

Cowboy hat? Absolutely. Both Fonda and husband Ted Turner won Golden Boots Awards for their work in westerns.

1994

The narrator of A Century of Women attended the New York City premiere with her layered locks worn loose.

2001

The actress/activist sported a new pixie cut courtesy of super stylist Sally Hershberger when she was honored with a Lincoln Center Gala Tribute.

2005

After a 16-year break from the big screen, a radiant Fonda returned to the red carpet for the premiere of Monster-In-Law. This feathered, piecey pixie with dimensional blonde color is simply flattering.

2010

Auburn locks and a few extra tweaks. "I thought it would be good to have something minimal done," Fonda told People of her very public plastic surgeries. "I wanted to look less tired."

2011

Back to blonde, the A-lister was looking better than ever at the Cannes Film Festival premiere of Pirates of the Caribbean: On Stranger Tides.

2012

At a Golden Globes after-party, Fonda opted for shimmery eye makeup, pink gloss, and a swipe of blush to achieve a balanced beauty look.

2014

Shimmery shadow and glossy lips finished Fonda's look at the This Is Where I Leave You premiere in L.A. Meanwhile, feathered layers highlighted her face and added a note of '70s flavor.

2015

Fonda was dressed to the nines while attending the 46th Anniversary Gala Vanguard Awards in Los Angeles where she was an honoree. Pearlescent eyeshadow and lengthy lashes brought the focus of her look to her blue eyes.

2016

The Grace and Frankie star went all-out glam, favoring sparkles and shine in this beauty look replete with silvery shadow and a glossy peach lip. Her blonde bob, styled in volumized curls, frames her face to a T.

2017

The celebrated actress arrived at the premiere of Our Souls at Night with her blonde locks swept back, a pale pink lip, and bold liner, demonstrating the power that lies in simplicity.

2018

Attending the 1st Annual Environmental Media Association Honors Benefit Gala, Fonda stepped out in frosted makeup and a voluminous bob.

2019

The silver siren channeled Old Hollywood with retro curls and a crimson red lip at the 25th Annual Screen Actors Guild Awards, leaving us breathless.

2020

2020 marked a momentous year for Fonda who decided to surprise us all (and we mean that in the best way possible) by embracing her grays. The result was not only refreshing but also absolutely stunning.

2021

At the Women in Film's Annual Award Ceremony, feathered layers added movement and dimension to Fonda's cut and color. Her matte peach lipstick lent a natural flush.

2022

The timeless beauty embodies simple yet sophisticated style. A gorgeous gray pixie, minimal makeup, and LBD make her red-carpet royalty.
