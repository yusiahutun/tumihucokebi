Midsomer Murders cottage on the market for 1.3million

March 26, 2024
blog

Not many people can boast of having had DCI Tom Barnaby examining evidence in their sitting room after a murder was committed in their front garden. 

But that's exactly what Brian and Zoe Bowman, who own the Grade II listed Hall House in the Buckinghamshire village of Haddenham, can claim.

In 2004, the cast and crew of ITV's Midsomer Murders – known for its picturesque locations – took over the timber-framed house to film an episode of the long-running series, called Things That Go Bump In The Night.

In the episode, a friend of Joyce, DCI Barnaby's wife, is killed when she is struck from behind with a brick while making her way into her home.

Crime scene: Hall House in Haddenham, Buckinghamshire, was used to film an episode for Midsomer Murders

'Fortunately, it was made of foam and looked incredibly realistic!' says Zoe, holding the brick which the production company left the Bowmans as a souvenir.

In the next scene, DCI Barnaby is seen stepping over the body to enter Hall House, and in another, he and Joyce walk through the garden and discuss the case in the sitting room, where Joyce had been with the murder victim the night she was killed.

'The production company who make the series knocked on our front door one morning to ask if we would mind our home being featured in Midsomer Murders, and we readily agreed,' says Zoe. 

Until 2011, John Nettles played the lead role of Tom Barnaby and Jane Wymark was Joyce. Now the lead character is Tom's cousin, DCI John Barnaby, played by Neil Dudgeon. 

Spacious: The six-bedroom cottage boasts a 24ft drawing room with a vaulted ceiling and minstrels' gallery

At the time of the Bowmans' episode, Barnaby's sidekick was DS Dan Scott, played by John Hopkins.

Zoe adds that Hall House, which dates from the 14th century, has often been in Midsomer Murders. 'They've filmed at our house on a number of occasions, although we've never met any of the cast or crew, as we're always on holiday when they come to film.'

Hall House, which is on the market for £1,350,000, is the former home of Conservative MP Sir John Stokes, from whom the Bowmans bought it 20 years ago. 

HALL HOUSE: AT A GLANCE

Price: £1.35 million

Location: Haddenham, Buckinghamshire

Bedrooms: Six

Unique features: Grade II listed, 4,000 sq ft living area, once home of MP Sir John Stokes, Midsomer Murders location

Sir John was once described as 'Parliament's last link with the Middle Ages' because of his uncompromising views. 

Since the Bowmans bought the property, they have renovated a barn and incorporated the building into the house, creating a 24ft drawing room with vaulted ceiling and minstrels' gallery. 'We were told that the barn could have been part of an old monastery from before the Norman Conquest,' says Brian.

The house, which has a large number of exposed beams, has a living area of almost 4,000 sq ft, which includes six bedrooms, three bathrooms and four reception rooms. It stands opposite the village green and duck pond in Haddenham, which is between Aylesbury and Thame.

Haddenham is mentioned in the Domesday Book and its church, St Mary's, was built in the 13th Century. Hall House is approximately one mile from Haddenham railway station, which has direct access to London Marylebone in approximately 40 minutes. The M40 is about five miles away.

Starring role: DCI Tom Barnaby, played by John Nettles, and DS Scott, played by John Hopkins, investigated a murder at the cottage

Brian, 73, previously worked as a civil district judge and Zoe, 70, used to run an outside catering company. The couple, who have been married for 50 years and have two daughters and five grandchildren, are looking to downsize to a smaller house in Goring-on-Thames, where one of their daughters lives.

'We will be sad to leave the house as we've lived here since 1995 and everyone is incredibly friendly,' says Zoe.

'There's always something happening in the village, including a village fair, maypole dancing on the green, and weddings in the parish church most weekends.

'It's lovely to hear the church bells ringing and children playing on the green, and we open our house during the village fair for teas, coffees and cakes to raise money for the MS Society, which we support.'
