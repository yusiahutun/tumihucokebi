Harvard sophomore Luke Tang dies 'suddenly' less than 2 weeks into fall semester

April 18, 2024
blog

A Harvard College sophomore was found dead early Saturday in what school officials are calling a 'sudden and unexpected tragedy.'

The passing of Luke Tang, 19, is being investigated by police, but Dean of Harvard College Rakesh Khurana said no foul play is suspected.

Middlesex District Attorney's Office spokeswoman Elizabeth Flock confirmed that the incident was not considered suspicious. It remains unknown at this time where the 19-year-old was found, at what time Saturday or who made the disturbing discovery.

High achiever: Tang studied physics at Harvard College (pictured), served on the board of the Harvard Interfaith Forum and was a church teacher on campus 

Luke Tang's untimely death was announced in an email that was sent out to students and staff at the prestigious college this weekend.

‘This is a very difficult time for those who knew Luke, including his family and friends. Our thoughts and prayers are with each of them,’ Khurana said. ‘Such a sudden and unexpected tragedy is shocking and evokes strong feelings in all of us, even if we did not directly know Luke.’

The college said it was not releasing the cause of death or any additional details on the circumstances of Tang's passing out of respect for the privacy of his family.

An obituary published in the New Orleans Times-Picayune described the teenager as a standout student who was named Benjamin Franklin High School’s student of the year and salutatorian in 2014.

That same year, Tang was also named a US Presidential Scholar.

High honor: Tang was named Benjamin Franklin High School’s student of the year and salutatorian in 2014

Standout: Luke, pictured as a young boy left and as a high school student right, taught violin to children and served as concertmaster for the Greater New Orleans Youth Orchestra.

In his free time, Tang taught violin to underprivileged children and served as concertmaster for the Greater New Orleans Youth Orchestra.

During his freshman year at Harvard, where he was studying physics, Tang became a school teacher at Memorial Church, served on the board of the Harvard Interfaith Forum and was a member of the Asian American Christian Fellowship at the Ivy League university.

The son of Chinese immigrants who arrived in the US in 1990, Tang came from a family of high achievers.

His father, Dr Wendell Tang is a pathologist a Ochsner Medical Center who earned his doctorate from the University of Hawaii; his mother, Christina Tang, is a certified public accountant who works as a senior financial analyst at the Children's Hospital in New Orleans.

Luke's older brother, Richard, is also a gifted musician who is currently studying medicine at Tulane University.

Harvard’s Lowell House dormitory (pictured), where Tang lived, hosted a standing-room-only memorial in honor of the late sophomore

On Saturday evening, Harvard’s Lowell House dormitory, where Tang lived, hosted a standing-room-only memorial in honor of the late sophomore.

‘This was a profound testament to how important he had been in the short time he was at Harvard,’ house Master Daniel Eck told The Harvard Crimson. 'There is such a strong group of students who are sophomores who know him, knew him, loved him, and will continue to remember him.'
