Wesley Zachary On Tex Mex Motors

Car restoration shows have found a niche in the market. No wonder, after the grand success of shows like Car Masters and Drive Hard, Netflix is back with another masterpiece, and it’s titled Tex Mex Motors. As per their official synopsis, Tex Mex Motors is bringing “junk” automobiles into a workshop to be turned into “jewels.”

Overall, the show follows a team in a shop right on the border of Mexico and the USA, in El Paso, Texas, intending to clear $250,000 before going deeper into Mexico to find and refurb even more cars.

The shop is run/owned by Mike Coy where Wesley works as the team’s master fabricator.

Mike describes him as “kind of a quiet genius”.

Other members of Tex Mex Motors include Robert ‘Rabbit’ Pitts (sales guru), Scooter Wreyton (car scout), Jenicio Adame (rookie), and Jaime Hjelm (engine and electrical specialist).

How Much Is Wesley Zachary Net Worth?

Wesley Zachary flaunted a net worth of above $800 thousand by 2023.

Working on cars since the age of 5, Wesley officially started his career in the 90s, building, and racing cars, and simultaneously working on his craft of metal fabrication.

Fast forward to August 2020, the fabrication expert then opened his own shop named  Zachary Customs. 

“What sets us apart is artistry in metal fabrication and the creative vision of the overall build. Our focus and mission is your project, giving attention to detail combined with purposeful thought in the build plan. Our goal is for you to drive your dream car with confidence and pride,” he wrote.

At Zachary Customs, Wesley and his team offer skilled mig and tig welding services. Besides offering solid welds, the team also specializes in custom-designed dashes and engine bay panels. “The strength of our welds adds confidence in our four link, Mustang II, subframe connector and C knotch professional installs. We install full and mini tubs that add the ability to put more rubber to the road. Our panel replacement and rust repair brings new life and a strong foundation to classic restorations,” they boasted.
