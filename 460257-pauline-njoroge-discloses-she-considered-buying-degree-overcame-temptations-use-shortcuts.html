Pauline Njoroge Discloses She Considered Buying Degree: &quot;Overcame Temptations to Use Shortcuts&quot;

June 26, 2024
blog
  • In an era where people prefer to use shortcuts instead of going through university, blogger Pauline Njoroge says she nearly took the same route
  • She, however, overcame the temptation and enrolled on a degree course followed by a Master's immediately after
  • As much as it was a long and treacherous path, she is proud that her academic qualifications are legitimate

Political blogger Pauline Njoroge has revealed that in early 2014, she considered buying a degree certificate.

This, she said, was due to the fact that she was not earning enough to educate both herself and her younger sister at the same time.

That meant that she had to shelve her dream of going back to university until her junior was done.

It was a development that made her lose hope in the future as she would miss opportunities because of a lack of papers.

"I thought the easiest way out was to save KSh 100,000 and shop for an institution that would forge the certificate for me," she said.

Esther Juma botched her plans

Pauline says that everything seemed to be going according to plan until she shared her intentions with Esther Juma.

Instead of supporting her, Esther rebuked her and told her to trust God and pray for His provision instead of trying shortcuts and committing a crime.

"We prayed and committed the matter to God. Long story short, by the end of that year someone gave me KSh 100,000 to enrol for a degree course and pay for the first semester," she continued.

That is how in January 2015, she embarked on her bachelor’s degree in mass media and communication.

Tempted to buy degree again

Contrary to an earlier attempt where she had enrolled but dropped out of another degree course due to fees, this time things went smoothly.

In 2017, Pauline was tempted yet again to buy academic papers after she was offered a very good communications job as an expatriate with a salary of over $7000 (KSh 820,000).

"There was a requirement for a master’s degree but in my case, they were willing to overlook it because there was expertise they were looking for, which I had. Problem was, that I hadn’t completed my first degree," she noted.

When she consulted a colleague about the matter, he advised her to simply buy papers so as not to miss out on the opportunity of a lifetime.

Turned down lucrative job

Pauline says that, with benefit of hindsight, she figured that God had already shown her He was in control, therefore, her conscience could not allow her.

After gathering her thoughts, she called the people who were offering her the job and informed them that she was still in the process of acquiring her bachelor’s degree.

"They were quite shocked and disappointed because they had assumed I had a first degree in the least. But on my part, I was relieved to have told the truth," says Pauline.

Her patience paid when she graduated in August 2018 and enrolled for a master’s degree in international studies the following month.

Pauline says it was worth the sacrifice

In 2020, Pauline says she applied for a job under the African Union Development Agency (AUDA-NEPAD) headquartered in South Africa and was picked.

It was a double blessing for her the same year as she graduated with a master’s degree.

She reveals that there is so much joy and freedom in knowing that she did things right and the sacrifices she made.

"Being in class almost every weekday evening and Saturdays for almost six years was not in vain. God has faithfully repaid me for the opportunities lost," she noted.

Overall, she says that she is forever grateful that she choose to trust God in her education journey and overcame the temptations to use shortcuts.

Kenyan politicians and fake degrees

Her sentiments come at a time a number of politicians have hit headlines after question marks were raised about their academic papers.

The most highly-publicised case is of Nairobi gubernatorial aspirant Johnson Sakaja who has struggled to clearance from the Independent Electoral and Boundaries Commission (IEBC).

Sakaja has maintained that he has a degree while oscillating between Team University in Uganda and the University of Nairobi.
