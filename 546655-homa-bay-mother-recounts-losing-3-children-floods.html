Homa Bay Mother Recounts Losing 3 Children to Floods

April 19, 2024
blog
  • The flooding situation in Kenya has led to the death of about 200 people, with several others still missing
  • Benta Anyango said the floods had pierced her heart after waters swept away her three children
  • Anyango, 39, said the Sunday incident in Sindo had left her helpless and appealed for help to bury her children and treat her hospitalised husband

Many Kenyans are counting the immeasurable losses caused by floods experienced in most parts of the country.

Floods in Sindo

For Benta Anyango, a Sindo, Homa Bay county resident, the floods had robbed her of priceless gifts that she could never make up for.

The 39-year-old sat helplessly as she watched a police car ferry the bodies of her three children to the mortuary.

According to the mother of five, the raging waters that swept away over 110 houses in the Sindo area killed her three children and seriously injured her husband, who was still recuperating at Kisii Teaching and Referral Hospital.

Floods break Homa Bay mother's heart

She said that the waters swept away her children, aged 12, eight, and five, during the Sunday, April 28 incident.

They were in the company of her husband, whose efforts to rescue them did not bear fruit as he also escaped death by a whisker.

"I have lost my three children. All my property including my children's learning material and other basic items are gone. Apart from my two children and husband, I have nothing to call mine as waters swept away our home and loved ones," she said.

She said that the incident occurred when she had gone to do casual jobs in the neighbourhood.

"I had gone to fend for the family and it began raining. The rainfall was heavy, and I had to seek shelter, waiting for it to reduce before heading home. I didn't know the same rain would lead to the death of my children," Anyango narrated amid tears.

Aftermath of Sindo floods

She told TUKO.co.ke that the waters also swept away the money she had saved as her children's school fees.

"I am stranded. I don't know where to start. Now, my children are in the morgue and need to be buried honourably. How I will take the remaining to school is another breaking story," she narrated.

Anyango appealed to well-wishers and the government to help her bury her children and take the rest to school when the second term begins next week.

"I urgently need a roof over my head, even as I plan to bury my babies. The two surviving children are in Form One and Form Two and I'll need to pay fees to get them to school. I need help more because my husband is also in hospital," Anyango said.

Molly Onyango, a native of Rang'wa East sub-location within Sindo, was with Anyango on the farm on that fateful rain.

She said they sheltered at her home, hoping the rain would reduce.

"The rain could not stop, and she (Anyango) decided to go and check on her children, who could be so lonely at that time, and I accompanied her. Just close to the river bank next to her home, we saw one of her children covered by stones and tree logs," Onyango recounted.

Onyango recalled how she struggled to hold Anyango, who wanted to cross the swollen river to rescue her child.

"I had to pull her back and firmly hold her because the raging waters would have swept her too," she recalled.

John Odayo, Anyango's neighbour, explained that one of the children was swept to his home and was found dead after the heavy rains.

"It was shocking to find out that her child had been swept to my homestead by the waters. The waters swept away all my livestock too," Odayo narrated.

The country's flooding situation has caught the attention of leaders, including former president Uhuru Kenyatta, who pledged KSh 2 million to support the provision of humanitarian aid to affected Kenyans.

Flood death toll in Kenya

  • The government confirmed that at least four people were killed by floods in Sindo, Homa Bay county.
  • On the dawn of Monday, April 29, the Kijabe Dam in Mai Mahiu broke its banks and caused the deaths of at least 71 people.
  • Government spokesperson Isaac Mwaura announced on Monday, April 29, that the floods in the country had killed 103 people.
