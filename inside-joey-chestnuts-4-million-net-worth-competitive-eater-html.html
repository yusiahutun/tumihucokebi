Inside Joey Chestnut's $4 MILLION net worth as a competitive eater

October 06, 2024
blog

There's no denying that Joey Chestnut is one of the most successful competitive eaters of all time - and he'll be looking to secure his title at Nathan's Hot Dog Eating Contest this Independence Day.

The 39-year-old, from Westfield, Indiana, won the Fourth of July competition for a fifteenth time in 2022 when he devoured 63 dogs in 10 minutes.

However, it's not just hotdogs that he has a talent for eating - having set 55 world records in all kinds of food-related contests including eating 141 hard-boiled eggs in eight minutes and 32 Big Mac sandwiches in 38 minutes.

But his impressive eating habits are more than just a hobby as the former San Jose State University student now earns a hefty salary thanks to his bizarre talent. 

Champ: Competitive eater Joey Chestnut has won Nathan's Hot Dog Eating Contest 15 times

Food fanatic: There's no denying that 39-year-old Joey is one of the most successful competitive eaters of all time

Chestnut made the decision to leave his job with a construction management company in Northern California and become a full-time competitive eater in 2010.

Before making that decision, Pepto-Bismol offered him the chance to promote and participate in four competitive eating contests for $10,000 per event.

Chestnut took a month off of work in order to do so and, after being paid for the four events, he decided to quit his full-time job.

According to property records, Chestnut bought his first house in 2014 at the age of 30 for $618,000. 

Last year, he told USA TODAY Sports he earned more than $500,000 and his net worth exceeds $4 million. 

Speaking about maintaining his brand and success, he said: 'You have to see yourself as a business when you're working for yourself.

'But I never knew how far it would go,' he admitted.

In 2022, Chestnut signed with Everest Talent Management, who have secured him promotional deals with DUDE Wipes, Raising Cane's Chicken Fingers, Wonderful Pistachios and Pepsi.

A decent dollar: Joey's eating habits aren't just a hobby to him, as he now makes a living from competing

Wealthy guy: Last year, Joey said he earned more than $500,000 and his net worth now exceeds $4 million

Joey participates in all kinds of food-related contests and has set 55 other world records

Chestnut entered the competitive eating scene in 2005 with a break-out performance in the deep-fried asparagus eating championship.

During this contest, he beat high-ranked eater Rich LeFevre by eating 6.3 pounds (2.9 kg) of asparagus in just 11.5 minutes.  

That same year, during Nathan's Hot Dog Eating Contest, he ate 32 HDB, and came in third place behind Takeru Kobayashi and Sonya Thomas.

Elsewhere in 2005, Chestnut defeated Thomas in the Waffle House World Waffle Eating Championship and placed second to Kobayashi in a Krystal Square Off World Hamburger Eating Championship qualifier, eating 56 Krystal Burgers in eight minutes to his 67.

He later beat him by eating 91 hamburgers in the finals, finishing second to the 97 hamburgers consumed by Kobayashi.

Chestnut didn't actually win Nathan's Hot Dog Eating Contest until 2008 when he tied with Kobayashi.

Their tie resulted in a five HDB eat-off, which Chestnut won by consuming all five before Kobayashi.

The only time since then that Chestnut has been defeated on July Fourth was back in 2015 when fellow competitive eater Matt Stonie consumed 62 hot dogs and buns, ending Chestnut's eight-year run as champion.

Back on top: Matt's victory didn't last long as Joey redeemed himself in 2016 and ate 70 hot dogs and buns in total, while Matt managed 53

Girl power: Miki Sudo (left) is also a reigning champion of the July 4 event and has won the contest eight times from 2014 to 2022

However, Stonie's victory didn't last long as Chestnut redeemed himself in 2016 and ate 70 hot dogs and buns in total, while Stonie only managed 53.

Chestnut has confirmed that most of his income is generated by contest earnings, paid appearances and endorsement deals.

Most recently, he won the first-ever Qdoba World Burrito Eating Championship in Milwaukee, Wisconsin, consuming 14.5 burritos in 10 minutes and pocketed $5,000.

Back in February 2020, he posted a photo of himself on Instagram holding a check for $1,500 after he broke a new record in New Orleans in the Red Beans and Rice eating competition.

He stands to win $10,000 on July Fourth if he manages to win Nathan's Hot Dog Eating Contest again, but Chestnut also competes to raise money for various charities.

In December 2020, Chestnut and Miki Sudo raised over $20,000 for charities, including Gleaners Food Bank of Indiana, when they took part in a shrimp cocktail eating competition at St. Elmo Steak House in Indianapolis.

Miki is also a reigning champion of Nathan's Hot Dog Eating Contest and has won the contest eight times from 2014 to 2022.

The 38-year-old holds the women's world record for most hot dogs eaten in 10 minutes with 48.5, which she set back in 2020, and will be vying for her ninth title come this July 4.

Speaking to DailyMail.com recently about how she had been preparing for the annual competition on Coney Island, Miki said she feels 'really calm.'

'[I've] been really mindful of what I've been eating, how I've been resting... spent some time in the gym.

'I've gone back and watched some footage and I think I've tweaked a few things to have a really strong performance this year.

'So, it's just 11 days to go, I think I'm going to try to do one more practice or do a half practice just to really get that technique dialed in and... it should be a high performance, a high number this year,' she added hopefully.
