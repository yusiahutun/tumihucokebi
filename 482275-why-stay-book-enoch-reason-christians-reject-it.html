Facts about the Book of Enoch

Contingent upon your religious affiliation, you might have heard about the Book of Enoch. So, what is the book all about, and why do different religious factions, including religious scholars, approach it with scepticism?

The Book of Enoch is a pseudepigraphical work (not included in any canon of scripture). The book’s only complete extant version is an Ethiopic translation of a previous Greek translation made in Palestine

It possesses apocalyptic accounts with visions and revelations about the end of times. It has five sections, which talk about the fall of angels, how Nephilim (referred to as giants in the Bible) arose, why the great flood took place, some astronomy and other prophecies.

Does this sound familiar? You cannot help but compare the book's writings with the Book of Revelations, the last in the New Testament. However, the difference is that the Book of Revelations was accepted and is in the Holy Scriptures, but Enoch is not. The Book of Enoch's main theme is judgment for the wicked and blessings for the righteous.

Who was Enoch in the Bible? The Book of Enoch has gained immense popularity because of its name, Enoch. He was the great-great-great-grandson of Adam. Genesis 5:22-24 states:

After he became the father of Methuselah, Enoch walked faithfully with God for 300 years and had other sons and daughters. Altogether, Enoch lived a total of 365 years. Enoch walked faithfully with God; then, he was no more because God took him away.

Why was the Book of Enoch removed from the Bible?

The Book of Enoch is not in the Bible. However, other people assume it is part of the Catholic deuterocanonical books, but it isn't. The book is popular and even quoted in other religious scripture, including the Book of Jude. So, what is the primary reason the book is not part of the Bible?

Enoch did not write it

Many scholars have agreed that the Books of Enoch is a compilation of different texts from different authors and was not composed by Enoch in the Old Testament. Historians date the oldest sections of the book to 300 B.C.

Scholars have also found out that the youngest sections date to 100 A.D. From the timeline, you can easily tell that it was written around 3000 to 4000 years after Enoch walked the earth.

The fact that the book's authors wanted to make it sound like Enoch wrote is also suspect. The writers used Enoch's name to attract attention and persuade people to adopt their view on a certain social matter.

God didn't inspire the book

Another major reason that the Book of Enoch is not in the Bible is that it doesn't demonstrate the marks of inspiration from God. It is popular, but that doesn't mean God inspired it. Even though a few early churches advocated for using the book in churches, mostly because Jude mentioned it, people later changed their minds about it.

The Book isn’t aligned with the Bible

Religious scholars have agreed that the Book of Enoch was not composed to teach about God or apply Biblical truth but for other purposes. For instance, the first section, The Book of Watchers, talks mostly about priestly marriage, which was controversial in the Jewish community.

Jews and Christians rejected the book

The Jewish community, before Christ, were aware of the Book of Enoch and did not accept it since it didn't meet the standards of being considered an inspired scripture. On the other hand, Christians have rejected it.

And the most interesting thing is that almost all branches of Christianity do not accept it. This includes the Roman Catholics Church, the Eastern Orthodox Church, Protestant Churches and many others.

The most significant reason the Book of Enoch is not in the Bible is that the Holy Spirit did not inspire it. On top of this, Jude quoting it doesn't make it inspired.

The book also goes against Christian teachings and has a reputation for paganism. It has false teachings that contradict what other books in the Bible teach and tries to create additional doctrines.
