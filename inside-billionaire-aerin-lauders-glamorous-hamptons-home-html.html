Inside billionaire Aerin Lauder's VERY glamorous Hamptons home

May 21, 2024

From stylish monochrome interiors to stunning table settings at every meal, Aerin Lauder's glamorous Hamptons home is exactly what you'd expect from from the billionaire heiress. 

American-born Aerin, 50, is isolating in a Greek revival house in East Hampton, which was built in the 1950s and originally purchased by her grandmother, Estée.  

Since lockdown began, the beauty heiress has offered a glimpse into her luxurious life on Instagram, revealing how she's mastered the art of tablescapes and sharing a look at the stylish bedroom of her late grandmother. 

Mother-of-two Aerin, who is estimated to be worth $2.3 billion, balances her time between her home in the Hamptons, a house in Aspen, Colorado, and an apartment in Paris.

Aerin is isolating with her husband Eric Zinterhofer, 48, and their two sons Jack, 20, and Will, 19, as well as her own parents Ronald, 76, and Jo. 

The beauty mogul married Eric in a Jewish ceremony in Wainscott, New York in 1996. He is an investment banker, co-founder of the private equity firm Searchlight Capital Partners.

Estée bought the home as she wanted to be near Aerin's parents, who often spent their weekends in the Hamptons - and the house, which boasts signature white columns favored by Estée, has since been purchased and renovated by the family.

The home boasts classic furniture, however Aerin has spruced up the traditional house with fresh layers of brighter paint and added various new home accessories.

Aerin's own eponymous brand was launched in 2011, first as a beauty collection and then later branching out to offer home furnishings, decor, and accessories.

The lifestyle label is said to have been established with the hope of encroaching on some of Tory Burch's polished, feminine-ware-seeking clientele. If offers the same look, with a more understated, sophisticated feel.

Aerin has been a longtime staple in the society pages of Vogue. She is so closely tied to the magazine that Anna Wintour helped provide guidance on her namesake label's launch.

Her company's products are sold at premium retailers including Bergdorf Goodman, Saks and Neiman Marcus.
