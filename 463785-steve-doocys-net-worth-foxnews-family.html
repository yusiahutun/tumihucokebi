Early life

The news anchor was born Stephen James Doocy on 19 October 1956 (age 65 as of 2022) in Algona, Iowa, United States. He is the first child and only son of James Edward Doocy and Joanne (Bee Sharp) Doocy. He has four siblings (all sisters), namely Jenny Doocy, Lisa Doocy, Cathy Curtis, and Ann Walker.

Steve comes from a multiracial ethnicity. His maternal grandmother was of Swedish descent, and his paternal grandfather was of Irish descent. He grew up in a working-class family. His father worked in sales and construction and raised him in Industry, Kansas, United States.

He attended Kansas grade schools in Russell, Salina, and Industry. Then, he joined junior high school in Wakefield and high school in Clay Center, Kansas. After his high school education, Doocy enrolled at the University of Kansas in Lawrence.

He graduated several years later with a Bachelor of Science Degree in Journalism. Before graduating, Steve was the first on-air disc jockey for KJHK radio, a radio station operated by students in Lawrence.

Professional career

Doocy started his professional career in the late 1980s. His first job was on TV as a reporter at TV stations in Topeka, Des Moines, Wichita, and Kansas City. He then became a reporter for WRC-TV in Washington. D.C.

In 1990, he joined NBC and became the host for the network's nationally syndicated programme, House Party with Steve Doocy. Then, in 1994, he became a co-host for the morning programme Wake Up America on NBC.

Doocy left NBC and joined CBS-TV in 1996, where he anchored the morning newscast on WCBS-TV in New York City. In 1996, Steve joined the Fox News Channel. He became a co-host for the network's morning show, Fox & Friends, in 1998. Then, in 2004, he helped the network launch a New Year's Eve special programme, All American New Year.
