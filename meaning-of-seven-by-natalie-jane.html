Meaning of Seven by Natalie Jane

May 11, 2024
blog

The Natalie Jane song 'Seven' is a poignant exploration of the complexities of love and its aftermath. The overall theme of the song revolves around the idea of questioning the genuineness of a past relationship and the resulting emotional turmoil. Throughout the lyrics, Natalie Jane touches upon various aspects of love, heartbreak, and self-discovery, eliciting a range of emotions in the listener.One standout lyric that resonates with the overarching theme is "Was it ever really love if the night that we broke up, Both went out to go hook up, With the one we both told each other not to worry about?" This lyric delves into the idea of infidelity, trust, and the underlying insecurities that often affect a romantic relationship. It suggests that perhaps the love shared between the two individuals was never as strong or genuine as they both believed.Building upon this idea, another significant lyric is "Seven months was just a fling, I'm starting to freak out." This line underscores the fleeting nature of the relationship, highlighting the speaker's realization that what was once believed to be a substantial connection was, in fact, only a temporary affair. It leads to the exploration of the insecurities and anxieties that arise when one realizes the impermanence of a past love.Moreover, the lyric "Remember when you said you wanted me to go blonde, Well, now it makes sense that you ran right into her arms" encompasses themes of individuality and the pressure to conform to one's partner's desires. It sheds light on the speaker's realization that their ex-partner had specific preferences and attractions that might have led to the downfall of their own relationship. It explores the notions of ownership and the quest for identity within a romantic context.Another thought-provoking line is "I bet you think she's nice, I bet she's just your type." This lyric introduces the idea of comparison and self-devaluation. It suggests that the speaker cannot help but compare themselves to the person their ex-partner is now involved with, questioning their own worth and desirability. This introspective examination leads to self-doubt and a search for validation.The recurring post-chorus lyrics, "Ha, ha, ha, ooh, ooh, ooh, Was it ever really love? Ha, ha, ha, ha, ha-ha," function as a haunting reflection on the true nature of the relationship. It acts as a self-interrogation, questioning the authenticity of the emotional connection the speaker believed they shared with their ex-partner. The laughter intertwined with the lyrics adds an element of bitterness and cynicism, illustrating the wavering confidence and stability of the speaker's emotions.In conclusion, 'Seven' by Natalie Jane is a deeply introspective song that navigates the complexities of love, heartbreak, and self-discovery. Through its poignant lyrics, the song explores the doubts surrounding the authenticity of a past relationship, the insecurities that arise when confronted with the impermanence of a love once cherished, and the quest to reconcile one's own identity amidst the turbulence of heartbreak. It serves as a reminder that relationships can often be unpredictable, leaving us questioning our own perceptions and understanding of love.
