Longest river in Kenya: The top 5 list with photos and location

February 10, 2024

Kenya is home to several rivers that contribute to the country's ecosystem. From providing water for irrigation to supporting a rich biodiversity, these rivers are an integral part of Kenya's landscape. Besides sustaining people and wildlife, the rivers also offer a glimpse of the country's rich natural heritage. But which is the longest river in Kenya?

To determine the longest river in Kenya, we considered criteria such as its total length from source to mouth, flow rate, drainage area, geographic significance, and the consensus among geographical authorities.

By analyzing these factors comprehensively, we aimed to identify the river that plays the most significant role in Kenya's landscape, ecology, and human activities, providing a clear understanding of its importance within the country's geography and environment.

Which is the longest river in Kenya?

When discussing the longest river in Kenya, one river that crosses people’s minds is the River Nile. However, the River Nile does not directly pass into or flow through Kenya. Instead, it begins its epic journey in Lake Victoria, which straddles the borders of Kenya, Uganda, and Tanzania.

1. Tana River – 1,000 kilometres

The Tana River is the longest and the deepest river in Kenya. Its length is 1,000 kilometres, and it covers approximately 100,000 square kilometres. Its tributaries include some major rivers in the Central Region, including the Thika, Ragati River, Nyamindi, Thiba, Mathioya, and Chania River.

The Tana River is known for its extraordinary biodiversity. It is a crucial water source for irrigation and hydroelectric power generation and supports a diverse ecosystem along its banks.

The river sustains agriculture and livelihoods and provides tourism opportunities, with activities such as bird watching and river safaris attracting visitors from near and far.

The Tana River plunges over the Kitaru (Seven Forks) Falls, which is 134 meters high, into a semi-desert landscape that constitutes its middle course. The river then veers south and opens into a wide valley, which meanders through a floodplain.

Where is source of river Tana? The Tana River originates from the Aberdare Mountain Range and Mount Kenya. It then flows approximately 1,000 kilometres from its headwaters in the Aberdare Range and Mount Kenya to the Indian Ocean.

The river initially runs east before turning south around Mount Kenya's massif and meanders up to the Indian Ocean.

2. Ewaso Nyiro – 700 kilometres

Ewaso Nyiro, also known as Ewaso Ng'iro, is the second longest river in Kenya. The river is approximately 700 kilometres long and feeds into Lake Ol Bolossat. Also, the river gets its name from the local language, which means river of brown and muddy water.

The Ewaso Nyiro is a significant river in Kenya, originating from the western slopes of Mount Kenya and the Aberdare range. It flows north, east, and southeast, passing through Somalia, where it joins the Jubba River.

The river is a permanent river fed by seasonal rains and the glaciers of Mount Kenya. The Ewaso Nyiro River is vital in sustaining wildlife and supporting economic development and population growth.

It supports seven districts' arid and semi-arid lands, including Meru, Laikipia, Samburu, Isiolo, Wajir, Garissa, and Marsabit. The Ewaso Nyiro River is a lifeline for many species of wild animals and supports the Samburu National Reserve, Shaba National Reserve, and Buffalo Springs National Reserve.

Its waters guarantee the survival of wildlife in these areas, creating oases of green in an otherwise arid landscape. The river also supports agricultural development along its banks, providing a reliable water source for irrigation.

3. Mara River – 395 kilometres

The Mara River is one of the major rivers in Kenya. It is a 395-kilometre-long river that flows through Kenya's Narok County and Tanzania's Mara Region. The river originates from the Mau Escarpment in Narok County and drains into Lake Victoria in Tanzania.

The Mara River basin covers 13,504 square kilometres, with approximately 65% located in Kenya and 35% in Tanzania. The river is a vital water source for the wildlife in the Maasai Mara/Serengeti ecosystem.

It is famous for being a barrier to wildebeest migration, particularly during the middle of the year. Furthermore, the river is known for its role in the annual migration of thousands of wildebeests, which cross the river as part of their natural migration pattern.

The Mara River basin is home to diverse wildlife, including the big five animals, giraffes, crocodiles, and various bird species. The river's flow is affected by bi-modal rainfall seasons, with the long rains occurring from mid-March to June and the short rains from September to December.

The river's importance extends beyond its ecological significance, as it is also functionally and ecologically related to the socio-economic activities in Lake Victoria and along the river.

4. Athi-Galana-Sabaki River – 390 kilometres

The Athi-Galana-Sabaki River originates in the Gatamaiyo Forest as the Athi River. It flows southeastwards before meeting the Nairobi River near Thika, forming the Fourteen Falls.

After passing beneath the Yatta Ridge, the river continues its northeastern trajectory, eventually joining forces with the Tsavo River and adopting the dual identities of the Galana and Sabaki Rivers.

The Athi-Galana-Sabaki river system winds through various landscapes, crossing the fertile Kapute and Athi Plains, the dry Tsavo West National Park, and ultimately emptying into the Indian Ocean.

Along its route, the Athi-Galana-Sabaki River supports diverse ecosystems, providing habitats for hippopotami, crocodiles, and avifauna, particularly at the Sabaki Point estuary, recognised as an important bird area.

The river sustains millions of Kenyans through drinking water and agriculture. It contributes to economic development via initiatives like the Thwake Dam.

How long is Athi River? The Athi River spans approximately 390 kilometres before entering the Indian Ocean. It is the nation's fourth-longest waterway, draining an expansive area of approximately 70,000 square kilometres.

5. River Turkwel – 340 kilometres

The Turkwel River flows from Mount Elgon on the Kenya-Uganda border to Lake Turkana, a length of approximately 340 kilometres. The name Turkwel is derived from the Turkana name for the Tir-kol, which means a river that withstands the wilderness.

The Turkwel River’s flow is seasonally varied, subject to flash floods in the rainy season. Nevertheless, the river's significance extends beyond its natural features. It plays a crucial role in the local ecosystem and communities.

The river provides water for drinking and farming, particularly in semi-arid climates like West Pokot and Turkana County.

The Turkwel River supports numerous community farms, such as the Katilu Irrigation scheme and the Namakat community farm. Additionally, it is essential for local livelihoods, as communities rely on it for water for home use and farming.

Wrapping up

The longest river in Kenya is the Tana River. Other rivers that follow it include Ewaso Nyiro, Mara River, Athi-Galana-Sabaki River, and River Turkwel.
