Who's who in the royal box at Wimbledon? Anna Wintour brushes shoulders with David Harewood

June 25, 2024
blog

Today marked the official start of the second week of Wimbledon - and high profile tennis fans were out in full-force to catch a glimpse of some of the action on Centre Court.

US Vogue editor Anna Wintour brushed shoulders with actor David Harewood as well as members of European royalty, high-flying politicians and members of the Lawn Tennis Association.

This afternoon, Novak Djokovic beat Hubert Hurkacz in four sets before World No 1 Carlos Alcaraz went up against Matteo Berrettini.

Last week, the Princess of Wales was one of the exclusive attendees in the best seats in the house, alongside eight-time men's Wimbledon champion Roger Federer and his wife Mirka, a former professional tennis player herself. 

Today, the Royal Box has been graced by more famous faces. Here, FEMAIL takes a look at another star-studded line-up watching the action on Centre Court. 

The second week of Wimbledon got underway today - and high-profile tennis fans flocked to the Royal Box

The Evening Standard's Managing Editor (third row) Jack Lefley was pictured with his wife Sophie (left) in the Royal Box. MP Stuart Andrews pictured in the bottom left corner

1. Stuart Andrews 

Stuart Andrews is the MP for Pudsey and a politician serving as Parliamentary Under-Secretary of State for Sport, Tourism, Heritage and Civil Society.

He is openly gay, but he was not joined by his long-term partner, Robin Rogers.

2 & 3. Jack Lefley and wife Sophie

Jack Lefley is the managing editor of the Evening Standard, and he was joined by his wife Sophie.

Jack was acting editor of the Standard until recently, having took over the role last year from Charlotte Ross. She had followed on from Emily Sheffield - the sister of Samantha Cameron - and George Osbourne.

He was previously the deputy editor and head of news at the paper. 

4. Crown Prince Frederick of Denmark

Crown Prince Fredrick of Denmark, 55, was granted a front row seat in the Royal Box this afternoon

Crown Prince Frederick of Denmark, 55, took a seat front and centre of the royal box.

The heir to the Danish throne, who is married to Australian-born Princess Mary, opted for a simple suit and tie combo as he watched on.

Like King Charles, Frederick is the great-great-great grandson of Queen Victoria, making the pair fourth cousins. 

His mother is Margrethe II, the current ruling monarch of Denmark, and the only living Queen in the world.

He is very devoted to his Tasmanian wife Mary, recently penning a sweet dedication to her.

And despite his happy face today, it's been a tough year for the Crown Prince, after publicly falling out with his brother.

 The Danish royals put on a united in May to mark the 83rd birthday of Queen Margrethe - who underwent back surgery in March -at Amalienborg Palace in Copenhagen.

Crown Prince Frederick seen speaking with Dr Christian Buchwald as Wimbledon entered its second week

It was a tumultuous end to 2022 for the Danish Royal Family after the monarch stripped Prince Joachim's four children of their prince and princess titles - a shocking move which he publicly spoke out against.

She has said the decision was intended to allow Nikolai, 23, Felix, 20 - born from Joachim's first marriage - and Henrik and Athena to live normal lives without royal obligations.

Instead of being Princes and Princesses, they are Counts and Countess and referred to as Their Excellencies - a change which is now reflected on the household's website.

At the time, Joachim publicly spoke out against his mother's decision - claiming that his children had been 'harmed' in the process.

Later this year, the 53-year-old royal, who is sixth in line to the Danish throne, will relocate to Washington DC with his family to become defence industry attaché at the Embassy of Denmark.

5 & 6.  Ahmed Essam & Nadia Essam

Pictured: Ahmed Essam & Nadia Essam sitting in the third row of the royal box beside The Evening Standard's Acting Editor

Ahmed Essam is the CEO of Vodafone UK - one of the biggest sponsors of Wimbledon.

He took over the role in February 2021, after working as the  Group Chief Commercial Operations and Strategy Officer since 2018.

Previously, he'd worked as CEO of the Europe Cluster which includes Albania, Czech Republic, Greece, Ireland, Hungary, Portugal and Romania, and CEO of Vodafone Egypt from July 2014 to September 2016. 

Working his way up the telecoms company since joining in 1999, the businessman was joined by his wife Nadia.

7 & 9. Sir Trevor Phillips & Lady Helen Veale

Pictured: Sir Trevor Phillips, 69, was joined by his TV producer wife Lady Helen Veale at Wimbledon today

Sir Trevor Phillips, 69, is a broadcaster and writer who previously served as the Chair of the London Assembly.

The youngest of ten, Trevor was born in 1953 in Islington, three years after his family arrive in London for Guina.

He lived with his family in two rooms in a house in Finsbury Park, London, with another family upstairs, until his over-stretched mother sent him and a couple of siblings back to an aunt in British Guiana until he was six.

 He studied chemistry at Imperial College London, where he became president of the student union. He has been a writer, politician, broadcaster and dogged racial equality campaigner.

Currently, he works as a columnist for the Times and a presenter for Sky News.

He was joined by his wife, Helen Veale - also known as Lady Phillips - a television producer. 

8. Prince Michael of Kent

Pictured: Prince Michael of Kent, 81, seen sitting in the front row of Wimbledon's Royal Box today

Prince Michael of Kent, 81, is the first cousin of the late Queen and the son of George VI brother, Prince George, Duke of Kent.

The royal, who announced his retirement last year, is a keen tennis fan and often attends Wimbledon.

He is married to Princess Michael of Kent and the two and both active royals, living at Kensington Palace.

Last year the pair revealed they are to retire from royal life, months after Michael stepped down as patron of the Russo-British Chamber of Commerce, and handed back an Order of Friendship award, one of Russia’s highest honours.

The Queen’s first cousin is a fluent Russian speaker with a lineage to Tsar Nicholas II, was forced to sever his ties to Russia after its invasion of Ukraine.

According to the official Royal Family website, Prince Michael is classified as a ‘non-working royal’ and partakes in more than 200 public engagements for the not-for-profit sector, which are funded by his own household, rather than the taxpayer.

Princess Michael, who is nicknamed ‘Princess Pushy’ in some royal circles, is ‘actively involved in around 45 different charities and organisations’, according to the website, ‘including animal and wildlife trusts and health and welfare charities’.

Born in 1942, his first memorable appearance was as a five-year-old as a page boy at the Queen's wedding to Prince Philip.

While just outside the top 50 for succession to the throne, he is often seen at the side of the Queen at family events.

He has two siblings who he is often seen with, Princess Alexandra and the Duke of Kent.

10. Ian Hewitt 

Prince Michael of Kent seen speaking with Ian Hewitt, who is Chairman of the Wimbledon Foundation

Ian Hewitt, Chairman of the Wimbledon Foundation and Vice Chairman of The All England Lawn Tennis and Croquet Club.

Before joining the club he was a partner for 25 years at Freshfields, a leading international law firm, and has written many books about Wimbledon and its history.

He was Chairman of the Wimbledon Foundation since its formation in 2013 until January 2020. 

11. Birgitte, Duchess of Gloucester 

Pictured: Birgitte, the Duchess of Gloucester (left) seen speaking with a friend on Day Seven of Wimbledon

Birgitte, the Duchess of Gloucester, is married to late Queen's cousin Prince Richard of Gloucester.

The royal, 77, was born in Denmark as Birgitte van Deurs.

She met her future husband in 1965 while studying at the Bell School of Languages, at Cambridge University.

He was working as an architect but cut his career short when his older brother Prince William of Gloucester tragically died in a plane crash seven weeks after their marriage.

Richard then became the heir to his father, Henry, Duke of Gloucester- one of George VI's brothers.

Prince Richard and Birgitte had not expected to embark on a life of duty but did so after their marriage when he became the heir.

They have spent 50 years as working royals and visited charities and organisations carrying out hundreds of engagements together for sectors such as humanitarian issues, the environment and veterans.

These are listed in the daily Court Circular but rarely reported on in the media.

Second to Princess Anne, the Duke of Gloucester was named as one of the hardest-working royals.

12. Anna Wintour 

US Vogue editor Anna Wintour in the royal box on day eight of the 2023 Wimbledon Championships at the All England Lawn Tennis and Croquet Club

Anna Wintour is the editor-in-chief of American Vogue and Global Editorial Director of Condé Nast.

The British-born journalist, 73,  has become an international style icon, and is the brains behind many born trends, and the Met Gala.

Wintour has gathered countless accolades and titles over the years, including a damehood in 2017 and current roles as artistic director of Vogue's parent company Conde Nast, editor-in-chief at American Vogue and 'global content adviser' to the whole publishing group. 

Born in London in 1949, her father was a newspaper editor and her family linked to aristocracy – she is related to the 18th Century novelist Lady Elizabeth Foster, who became the Duchess of Devonshire.

She moved to New York in her 20s – returning for a stint to edit British Vogue – and quickly rose through the ranks thanks to 'Uncle Si', media baron Si Newhouse, whose family has owned Conde Nast and Vogue since the 1950s.

Spending most of her time in her Manhattan home, a four-storey townhouse in Greenwich Village, Wintour also has a sprawling holiday home on Long Island's waterfront.

She opens up the 40-acre estate to friends – including actors Damian Lewis and Benedict Cumberbatch – and family every summer.

13. Luke Wintour 

Anna Wintour was accompanied to Wimbledon by her stylish nephew Luke. The pair are regulars in the royal box

The nephew of the most powerful woman in fashion, its no surprise Luke Wintour is so well-dressed.

He's been by his aunt's side in the royal box on many occasions at Wimbledon.

He is currently working for the Frieght Theatre, with a short film being aired on BBC iPlayer.

14 & 15. David Harewood & Kirsty Handy 

Pictured: Actor David Harewood with his wife Kirsty Handy. The couple share two daughters and live in South London

David Harewood OBE, is a British actor known for his roles in Homeland, and Supergirl.

The star, 57, looked dapper in a navy suit with a blue tie and also wore some stylish black sunglasses.

Meanwhile, his wife Kirsty Handy looked effortlessly elegant in a white floral dress with a blazer draped over her shoulders. 

The couple - who married in 2013 - share two daughters together and live in Streatham, South London.

16. Lord Mervyn King

Pictured: Lord Mervyn King in the Wimbledon Royal Box with his wife Barbara Melander

Lord Mervyn King - who is a member of the House of Lords - was also in the Royal Box on Centre Court this afternoon. 

The British economist, 75, worked as the Governor of the Bank of England for 10 years from 2003.

He is also currently the Chairman of the Philharmonia. He was joined in the Royal Box by his wife Barbara Melander. 

17. Sandi Procter

The Lawn Tennis Association's President Sandi Procter (bottom right) was pictured in the front row of the Wimbledon box

Finally, the Lawn Tennis Association's President Sandi Procter was also spotted happily watching the Wimbledon action today.

Sandi is currently the second female president of the LTA and will serve in the role for the next three years.

The tennis fan previously worked as a PE teacher before becoming a coach. She was working as the manager of Bromley Tennis Centre when US Open winner Emma Raduncau was based there.
