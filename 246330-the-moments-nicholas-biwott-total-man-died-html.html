The very last moments of Nicholas Biwott, the Total Man, before he died

- After taking his breakfast, Biwott told his family that he was going to the hospital but slumped to the floor before leaving the house

- He was pronounced dead upona arrival at the Nairobi Hospital

- He was 77 years at the time and had worked in different capacities for the first three Kenyan presidents

Nicholas Biwott,a veteran politician whose life was enshrouded in mystery slumped in his house and was pronounced dead upon arrival to the hospital.

This was on Tuesday, July 11.

According to reports that TUKO.co.ke has gathered, Biwott had informed his family that he was unwell.

After taking his breakfast, the total man informed his people that he wanted to go to hospital for routine checkup.

He collapsed before he could leave the house.

It was at the Nairobi hospital that he was declared dead upon arrival. His death was confirmed by his daughter Esther Koimett.

He had been in and out of hospital and was battling cancer.

At his death, he was 77 years of age and had worked with the first three Kenyan presidents in different capacities. His career spanned well over four decades.

Biwott was born in 1940 in the Rift Valley and served as a public servant in the governments of Kenya’s first president Jomo Kenyatta and retired presidents Daniel Arap Moi and Mwai Kibaki.

He was one of the most powerful cabinet ministers who amassed wealth in controversial ways.
